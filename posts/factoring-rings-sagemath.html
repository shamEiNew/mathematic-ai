Factoring Finite Factor Rings with SageMath

June 5, 2022 • By Sham Algebra Code

Introduction

We can express a finite abelian group as a direct product of cyclic groups. In a similar fashion can we express the group of units for a commutative ring \( R \). It's a difficult question although we can answer this question in special cases. For example, when \( R \) is finite we know that the group of units of \( R \), \( U(R) \) is isomorphic to a direct product of cyclic groups.

Theorem 1: Let \( F \) be a finite field with \( p^n \) where \( p \) is prime. Then for any positive integer \( m \) we have:

$$ U(F[x]/(x^m)) \cong \mathbb{Z}_{p^n-1} \times \prod_{i=1}^s n(k_{i-1}-2k_i+k_{i+1}) * \mathbb{Z}_{p^i} $$

Algorithm in SageMath

SageMath is an open-source software for mathematics built on many open-source packages. Below is a function to define the base ring.

def base_ring():
    """
    Returns:
    base_ring: It is ring of the form F[x] where F is a field
    p_x: Product of irreducible polynomials in F[x]
    """
    size = 7
    #base ring F[x] where F is field with x as indeterminate.
    base_ring.<x> = PolynomialRing(GF(size))
    #p(x) as a product of irreducible polynomials in F[x]
    p_x =((x+4)^8)*(x^2+4)*(x^3+3)^2
    return base_ring, p_x
